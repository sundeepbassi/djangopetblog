About

Welcome to Pet star, a blog for all you animal lovers!

Hi everyone my name is Shanti and I wanted to create this place to meet like-minded people who share a love of all animals. This blog aims to help lift our spirits by reminding each other just how wonderful our pets are and how they have enhanced our lives in some way. I'm sure each and every one of you will have some story to tell as every animal is a character and some have had some amusing adventures. Some may have come from sad backgrounds but what I want us all to remember is how the love you each now give each other has transformed your lives.

A little bit about my background:

I've always loved animals ever since I was a child and couldn't bear it if I saw anyone being cruel to them. I remember I once used to rescue worms maybe when I was about four or five years old and set up a worm hospital. There was a girl down my street who hated them so I would rescue those she hadn't killed and took them back to the hospital to heal in a cardboard box full of grass. I think even at a young age I felt that animals were beings with sensitivities the same as you and I. It seemed natural to treat any being the same way you wish to be treated yourself, which is basically with kindness.

I grew up being fascinated by turtles, snakes, and monkeys and tried to get books on them in the library. I don't know why I was drawn to these creatures but these are the memories of the first animals I seemed to be drawn to.

My father always loved to watch any sort of wildlife programme. In hindsight as an adult, I think he preferred animals to humans! I think his love of nature influenced me as I grew up appreciating and wanting to learn about all the other species that live on planet Earth.

I didn't get my first pet until I was about 9 years old when my dad brought me home a little kitten, a beautiful tabby with Scottish wild cat markings. I had always wanted a cat that would love to cuddle and be stroked but my first two cats were anything but this! I realise it was all my fault as I used to tease them as kittens and they used to love to bite my hand with their little fangs and put their hind legs into spin drive, scratching my wrists with their claws. Of course, at that time it didn't hurt one little bit when they were kittens but that same instinct to play in the same manner as adult cats hurt with their dagger-like claws and very sharp teeth! On a few occasions, they even drew blood but I still loved them.

I don't think my cats wanted to be as affectionate to me as I wanted to be with them. I had to learn the hard lesson that a cat has a strong personality. It does its own thing and on its own terms. It made me learn that just because this animal was my pet it didn't mean that I was in control of it or owned it.

I was devastated when my first cat Jenny was killed by a dog. Maybe that's why I never thought of having a dog as that traumatic experience made me feel like they were savages and out of control. Of course, all of that changed almost 30 years later when I found myself enjoying taking a friend's dog for a walk or dog sitting for them over the years.

There were several dogs of different breeds that I bonded to over time and maybe that's why I am now a new dog mother.

This little creature has transformed my life the most. She arrived at my doorstep with my friend who had rescued her from a farmer. My friend asked if I could look after her for a week which then became a permanent situation. I had never had a dog before and had no idea how to look after one. But with the help of videos, I watched on YouTube about how to train a puppy she soon proved to be a very good dog despite some sleepless nights. When I first held her she was so small I could carry her in my scarf and many people would stop by in the street to admire her pretty blue eyes. Her name Bubba is unusual. It means in Sanskrit ‘the awakened one’- it was such a meaningful name given to her by my friend I felt that it was best not to change it.

Bubba is now Four years old. It’s amazing how much she has grown and I just can’t imagine my life without her now.

So that's a bit about me and I hope over time more people will share their stories with me on this website in celebration of all the animals that have touched our lives. I believe when we take care of animals we take care of ourselves and learn to become better and kinder human beings.
